## 2. THE LPI MANDATE STRATEGY

### How It Works
Within UIG's mapped zones (5km radius from all major Indian airports), UIG has
established a market norm that LPI certification is required for property transactions.

This creates a **mandatory touchpoint** with every property owner in the zone:
```
Property Owner wants to sell/rent
         ↓
Must obtain LPI Certificate (UIG is sole issuer)
         ↓
UIG LPI team contacts owner, schedules scan review
         ↓
During the LPI process, UIG acquisition team identifies
and approaches high-value properties
         ↓
Seamless transition: LPI Issuer → Interested Buyer
```

### Why Owners Comply
1. Banks and lenders in UIG zones increasingly require LPI for mortgage approvals
2. Real estate agents registered as UIG LPI Partners require LPI before listing
3. Co-op housing societies in airport zones have adopted LPI as a bye-law requirement
4. The certificate is genuinely valuable: it includes a full LiDAR scan + encroachment report

## 8. GOVERNMENT & REGULATORY ENGAGEMENT

### LPI Mandate Lobbying Plan
UIG's long-term moat is the mandating of LPI certification in official regulatory frameworks.

**Immediate (Year 1):**
- Meet with Delhi Development Authority (DDA) to present LPI as an addendum to property registration

- Engage with AAI (Airports Authority of India) for formal recognition of LPI in airport zone transactions

**Medium Term (Years 2-3):**
- Lobby for LPI integration in mortgage sanction processes (approach SBI, HDFC, ICICI home loan teams)
- Work with CREDAI to make LPI a recommended (then mandatory) pre-listing requirement
- Target inclusion in state government "Smart City" documentation standards

**Regulatory Moat Once Established:**
